Personal Trainer Waiver and Informed Consent Form

Built for solo trainers, hybrid coaches, and boutique studios that need a simple pre-session signoff.

Header

Section 1

Nature of Personal Training Services

I understand that personal training involves physical exercise instruction, programming, and coaching. Exercise carries inherent risks, including but not limited to muscle soreness, injury, cardiovascular events, or aggravation of pre-existing conditions.

Section 2

Assumption of Risk

  1. I voluntarily choose to participate in personal training services provided by the trainer or business listed above.
  2. I understand physical exercise carries inherent risks even when performed correctly.
  3. I am responsible for exercising within my own physical limits.
  4. I will communicate discomfort, pain, dizziness, or unusual symptoms immediately.

Section 3

Release of Liability

To the fullest extent permitted by law, I release, waive, and discharge the trainer, business, employees, agents, and facility partners from liability, claims, or damages arising from participation in personal training services, except in cases of gross negligence or intentional misconduct.

Agreement and Signatures

FAQ

Common trainer waiver questions

Does a waiver replace a PAR-Q or intake form?
No. The waiver handles acknowledgment of risk. Intake and PAR-Q handle medical context and readiness screening.
Can online trainers use a waiver too?
Usually yes. Remote coaching still involves exercise risk, so consent language and signoff still matter.
Is this legally complete as-is?
No. It is a useful starting structure, but local legal review matters if you want language that matches your jurisdiction and business setup.
Can I turn this into a fillable digital form?
Yes. Many trainers paste the content into Google Docs, Jotform, HoneyBook, Practice Better, or another onboarding tool.
