Header

Instructions

Physical Activity Readiness Questions

Ask the client to answer each question honestly. A "yes" answer does not automatically ban exercise, but it usually means you should slow down, clarify the issue, and consider medical clearance first.

Section 1

Cardiovascular and Symptom Checks

Has a doctor ever said you have a heart condition or advised supervised activity only?
Do you feel chest pain during physical activity?
Have you had chest pain while resting in the last month?
Do you lose balance because of dizziness, or have you lost consciousness recently?

Section 2

Joint, Bone, and Movement Concerns

Do you have a bone or joint problem that could worsen with exercise?
Have you had surgery, a major injury, or a rehab plan in the last 12 months?

Section 3

Medical and Lifestyle Factors

Are you taking prescription medication for blood pressure, heart issues, blood sugar, or another condition that affects exercise?
Are you currently pregnant, recently postpartum, or recovering from a major health event?
Is there any other reason you know of that you should not do moderate to hard exercise right now?

Section 4

Trainer Review and Sign-Off

FAQ

Common PAR-Q questions from trainers

Is a PAR-Q the same thing as an intake form?
No. Intake captures goals, history, and logistics. The PAR-Q is the screening step that helps identify whether exercise should be modified or cleared first.
If a client says yes to one question, should I refuse to train them?
Not automatically. It means you should review the issue carefully and often ask for medical clearance before progressing intensity.
Can online trainers use this too?
Yes. Remote coaching still needs a readiness screen before workouts are assigned.
What should come after the PAR-Q?
Usually the next documents are the waiver, the fitness assessment, and the package agreement. The paid pack includes all three.
